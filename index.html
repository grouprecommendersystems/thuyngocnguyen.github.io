About me

Currently I am a PhD candidate in the Faculty of Computer Science at Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, where my research focuses on conversational group recommender systems.

How to pronounce my name? My full name is Thuy-Ngoc Nguyen, which is pronounced as /twee-nock win/, but please call me Ngoc /nock/ for short - everyone does.

Research

Broadly speaking, my PhD research aims at exploiting an interactive approach to facilitate the group decision making process where the complex trade-off between the satisfaction of an individual and the group as a whole typically occurs.

The research consists of investigating and adapting preference elicitation methods, along with group recommendation generation and presentation to diverse scenarios of group dynamics.

I'm involved in research activities on recommender systems, user modeling, user experience of recommender systems, and human-computer interaction.

App

STSGroup, a chat-based group recommender system, primarily focuses on supporting the discussion stage, where group members' preferences can be elicited and shaped. Particularly, it facilitates the decision making process by allowing group members to join a group chat environment where they can express opinions through text messages and evaluations. Based on their actions, the system supports various tasks that the users are likely to undertake during the decision process, such as making comparisons or asking for recommendations.
